About Caenorhabditis angaria

Caenorhabditis angaria (ex-species 3) is a gonochoristic (male-female) species that is part of the Drosophilae super-group of Caenorhabditis species, with quite distinct morphology and behavior compared to C. elegans. Molecular divergence with C. elegans is similar to the fish-human distance. The closest known relative of C. angaria is C. castelli, another male-female species with which it can produce F1 hybrids. C. angaria is found in tropical regions, associated with palm and sugarcane weevils, Rhynchophorus palmarum and Metamasius hemipterus (Curculionidae) in Trinidad and Florida. Dauer larvae wave and are transported by adult weevils. The association is probably phoretic, although C. angaria can develop on dead weevils. A notable difference between C. angaria and C. elegans is the spiral male mating behavior of the former.
